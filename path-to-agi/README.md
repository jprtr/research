## ARC-AGI: The Benchmark That Matters

| Benchmark | Release | o3 / GPT-5.2 Score | What It Tests |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| **ARC-AGI-1** | 2019 | o3-preview achieved breakthrough | Novel skill acquisition from minimal examples |
| **ARC-AGI-2** | 2025 | GPT-5.2: 54% (Dec 2025) → **98%** (Apr 2026) | Visual reasoning, abstraction, pattern matching |
|| **ARC-AGI-3** | 2026 | **<1%** (~0.37% GPT-5.4) for all frontier models | **Interactive reasoning** — AI must ask clarifying questions |

**Key dynamic:** ARC-AGI-2 was "solved" within months. ARC-AGI-3 was introduced to be harder — and it humbled every frontier model. The gap between memorizable benchmarks and genuine reasoning is closing fast, but still exists.

## The Trajectory (What Changed in 2025–2026)

1. **Reasoning is the new frontier.** GPT-5/o3 introduced chain-of-thought reasoning. Claude 3.7 Sonnet introduced "hybrid reasoning." The step from pattern matching to step-by-step reasoning has been the biggest jump since the original Transformer.

2. **Agentic capability is accelerating.** SWE-bench (real coding) went from 30% to 88% in 18 months. Models now write, debug, and submit code autonomously.

3. **Multimodality is assumed.** GPT-5, Gemini 2.5, Claude 4 all handle vision, audio, and text natively. The "general" in AGI requires cross-modal reasoning — this is now standard.

4. **Scale is still the driver.** $100B+ annual AI infrastructure spend (from the five major labs collectively). The trajectory is scaling compute + data + algorithmic efficiency.

5. **ARC-AGI-3 shows the ceiling.** Even frontier models fail novel reasoning tasks that require genuine abstraction. The "last mile" to AGI is harder than the previous miles.

## Three Scenarios

| Scenario | Timeline | Probability | What It Looks Like |
|----------|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Fast Takeoff** | 2026–2027 | 20% | o4 or GPT-6 achieves broadly superhuman reasoning. Regulatory panic. Talent/wealth concentration. |
| **Gradual AGI** | 2028–2030 | 50% | Models get incrementally better at everything. No single "AGI moment" but by 2030, AI handles most cognitive work. |
| **Longer Horizon** | 2030+ | 30% | ARC-style reasoning remains hard. Scaling hits diminishing returns. Embodiment and real-world grounding become the bottleneck. |

## So What?

1. **The Overton Window has shifted.** In 2022, AGI was a research curiosity. In 2026, the CEOs of OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, xAI, and Meta agree it's 1–3 years away — regardless of whether they're right, this frames the regulatory, investment, and cultural environment.

2. **Skill acquisition is the bottleneck to watch.** ARC-AGI-3 exposed that frontier models still fail at genuine novel reasoning. The moment a model reliably passes ARC-AGI-3 without training on it is the moment to take "AGI imminent" seriously.

3. **Positioning:** If AGI is 1–3 years away, the value of learning "how to prompt" or "how to use AI tools" has a half-life. The durable skills become:
   - Problem decomposition and systems thinking
   - Domain expertise that AI cannot replicate (yet)
   - Infrastructure and deployment capability (where the models need humans to operate them)

4. **Investment implication:** The companies that win will be the ones wrapping AGI in usable products, not necessarily the ones building it. Open-source models are catching up. The moat is distribution, not model weights.

5. **Viability for a a venture concept:**
   - **AGI prep/consulting** — helping businesses get ready for agentic AI. Early, but TAM will explode if timelines hold.
   - **Local-first AI** — if AGI is cloud-dependent, there is a counter-position in privacy/self-hosted intelligence.
   - **Education/skills** — teaching "how to work with AGI" before everyone else. Short window but high value if timed right.
